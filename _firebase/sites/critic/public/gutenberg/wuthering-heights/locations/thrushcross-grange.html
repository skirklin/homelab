The elegant, refined home of the Linton family, larger and more comfortable than Wuthering Heights. Features a library, drawing-room with crimson carpet and furniture, parlour with windows overlooking gardens, and multiple bedrooms. Surrounded by a park with formal walking paths, buried in trees, representing civilization and culture.

Significance

Represents civilization, culture, and social refinement in contrast to Wuthering Heights' primitive wildness. The civilized world that Catherine Earnshaw enters through marriage, and later becomes the inheritance battleground for the next generation.
